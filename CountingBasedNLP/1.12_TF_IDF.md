# NLP Notes : TF-IDF (Term Frequency – Inverse Document Frequency)


## 1. Core Idea

**TF-IDF** is a numerical representation technique that measures how important a word is to a document relative to a collection of documents (corpus).

It builds on Bag of Words by adding **importance weighting**, not just frequency.

---

## 2. Problem

Bag of Words treats all words equally:

* High-frequency words dominate
* Common words (e.g., “the”, “is”) may get high importance
* Rare but meaningful words are undervalued

There is a need to:

* reduce importance of common words
* increase importance of distinctive words

---

## 3. How it Works

```text
Corpus
 → Compute Term Frequency (TF)
 → Compute Inverse Document Frequency (IDF)
 → Multiply TF × IDF
 → Generate weighted vectors
```

---

### Step 1: Term Frequency (TF)

Measures how frequently a word appears in a document.

$$
TF = \frac{\text{Number of times term appears in document}}{\text{Total number of terms in document}}
$$

---

### Step 2: Inverse Document Frequency (IDF)

Measures how rare or common a word is across all documents.

$$
IDF = \log \left( \frac{\text{Total number of documents}}{\text{Number of documents containing the term}} \right)
$$

* Log is used for normalization
* More common word → lower IDF
* Rare word → higher IDF

---

### Step 3: Final TF-IDF Score

$$
TF\text{-}IDF = TF \times IDF
$$

---

## 4. Examples

Corpus:

* "I love NLP"
* "NLP is powerful"

Word: "NLP"

* TF $doc1$ = 1/3
* TF $doc2$ = 1/3
* IDF = $log(2/2)$ = 0

👉 TF-IDF = 0 (word appears everywhere → not important)

---

Word: "powerful"

* TF $doc2$ = 1/3
* IDF = $log(2/1) $> 0

👉 Higher importance

---

## 5. Code Snippet

Using scikit-learn:

```python
from sklearn.feature_extraction.text import TfidfVectorizer

corpus = [
    "I love NLP",
    "NLP is powerful"
]

vectorizer = TfidfVectorizer()
X = vectorizer.fit_transform(corpus)

print(vectorizer.get_feature_names_out())
print(X.toarray())
```

---

## 6. Variations

* **Raw TF-IDF**
  Standard calculation

* **Sublinear TF Scaling**
  Uses log scaling for TF

* **Smoothed IDF**
  Avoids division by zero

* **N-gram TF-IDF**
  Uses phrases instead of single words

---

## 7. Limitations

* Ignores word order
* No semantic understanding
* Cannot capture context
* Sparse representation

---

## 8. Comparison

TF-IDF vs Bag of Words:

* Bag of Words:

  * Uses frequency only
  * Treats all words equally

* TF-IDF:

  * Weighs words by importance
  * Reduces effect of common words

---

## 9. When to Use / When Not to Use

Use when:

* text classification
* search engines
* document similarity

Avoid when:

* deep semantic understanding is required
* working with modern transformer models

---

## 10. Pipeline Placement

```text
Raw Text
 → Tokenization
 → Cleaning (Stopwords, Lemmatization)
 → TF-IDF Vectorization
 → Model
```

---

## 11. Use Cases

* Search ranking
* Document clustering
* Spam detection
* Information retrieval

---

## 12. Key Insight

TF-IDF improves upon simple frequency-based methods by balancing word importance across documents. Words that are frequent in a document but rare across the corpus are given the highest importance, making the representation more meaningful for machine learning tasks.

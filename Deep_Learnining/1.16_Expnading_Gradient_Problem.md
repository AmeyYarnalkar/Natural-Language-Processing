##  What it is (simple intuition)

During training, neural networks update weights using gradients:

$$
w_{new} = w_{old} - \eta \cdot \nabla L
$$

Now imagine this:

* Instead of gradients being reasonable (like 0.1, 0.01),
* they become **very large** (like 100, 1000, 10⁶…)

👉 That’s the **exploding gradient problem**

---

##  What actually happens

When gradients explode:

* Weight updates become **huge**
* Model becomes **unstable**
* Loss may:

  * jump wildly
  * become **NaN**
  * never converge

Think of it like:

> Instead of taking small steps toward the solution, the model starts **jumping randomly all over the place**

---

##  Why it happens (important for interviews)

This is the real core.

In deep networks (especially RNNs), gradients are computed using **chain rule**:

$$
\frac{dL}{dw} = \frac{dL}{da_n} \cdot \frac{da_n}{da_{n-1}} \cdot ... \cdot \frac{da_1}{dw}
$$

👉 You are **multiplying many gradients together**

Now:

* If values > 1 → they **grow exponentially**
* If values < 1 → they **shrink (vanishing gradient)**

So exploding gradient =
👉 repeated multiplication of values > 1

---

##  Where it commonly happens

* Deep neural networks (many layers)
* RNNs (very common)
* Poor weight initialization

---

##  Signs you are facing it

* Loss suddenly becomes **very large**
* Loss becomes **NaN**
* Weights become extremely large
* Training becomes unstable

---

##  How to fix it (this is what matters practically)

### 1. Gradient Clipping (most important)

Limit the gradient value:

```python
torch.nn.utils.clip_grad_norm_(model.parameters(), max_norm=1.0)
```

👉 Keeps gradients within a safe range

---

### 2. Better Weight Initialization

* Xavier initialization
* He initialization

---

### 3. Use Proper Activation Functions

* Avoid deep stacks of sigmoid/tanh
* Use ReLU / variants

---

### 4. Batch Normalization

* Stabilizes training
* Controls activation scale

---

### 5. Use architectures designed to handle it

* LSTM / GRU (for RNN problems)

---

##  Quick comparison (very important)

| Problem            | What happens            |
| ------------------ | ----------------------- |
| Exploding Gradient | Values become too large |
| Vanishing Gradient | Values become too small |

---

##  Final intuition (lock this in)

* Gradients are multiplied layer by layer
* If they grow → **explosion**
* If they shrink → **vanishing**

👉 Training fails in both cases

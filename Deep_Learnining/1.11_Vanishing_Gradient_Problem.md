# Why Sigmoid Causes Very Small Updates

## Step 1: What Sigmoid Does

Sigmoid:

* squashes values between **0 and 1**
* its derivative ranges between **0 and 0.25**

Important:

> The derivative is **always less than 1**

---

## Step 2: Where Derivatives Are Used

During backpropagation, gradients are computed like:

dL/dw = (chain of derivatives)

That means:

> You keep multiplying derivatives layer by layer

---

## Step 3: What Happens with Sigmoid

Since each sigmoid derivative is:

* between **0 and 0.25**

When you go backward through layers:

* multiply 0.2 × 0.1 × 0.05 × ...

👉 This quickly becomes **very small**

---

## Step 4: Why It Gets Smaller and Smaller

This is the key answer to your question:

> Why do values keep shrinking as they move backward?

Because:

* Each layer contributes a value **< 1**
* Multiplying many numbers < 1 → results shrink exponentially

Example:

* 0.5 × 0.5 = 0.25
* 0.25 × 0.5 = 0.125
* 0.125 × 0.5 = 0.0625

👉 It keeps reducing rapidly

---

## Step 5: Role of Learning Rate

You also mentioned learning rate.

That makes it even worse:

* Gradient is already very small
* Multiply by small learning rate

👉 Final update becomes **almost negligible**

---

## Step 6: Intuition (Very Important)

Think of it like passing a signal through many filters:

* Each filter reduces the signal slightly
* After many filters → signal almost disappears

---

## Step 7: What This Problem Is Called

This is known as:

> **Vanishing Gradient Problem**

---

## Step 8: Why It’s a Big Issue

* Early layers (closer to input) get **almost zero updates**
* They stop learning
* Model becomes ineffective for deep networks

---

## Final Understanding

* Sigmoid derivative is small
* Backprop multiplies many small values
* Gradients shrink layer by layer
* Updates become negligible

---

## One Line Summary

Because sigmoid derivatives are always less than 1, repeated multiplication during backpropagation makes gradients shrink rapidly, leading to vanishing gradients.
